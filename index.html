Nihal Ahmed

Quantifying the pathways to a sustainable future: from carbon peaking to ecological security, using econometrics, deep learning, and cross-country evidence.

Nihal Ahmed is an environmental economist dedicated to measuring and modelling ecological security, land‑use transitions, and carbon neutrality. His research fuses deep learning with spatial econometrics to evaluate how policies shape green economic restructuring, from China’s provincial carbon targets to cross‑border energy indices in BRICS and Nordic countries.

His core work revolves around building novel composite indicators of energy‑environment synergy and applying neural network approaches to land ecological security. With a PhD underway at Northwest A&F University, Nihal has authored over 18 journal articles in high‑impact environmental and energy journals, consistently bridging rigorous data science with actionable sustainability policy.
