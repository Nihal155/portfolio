About me

Environmental and agricultural economist exploring the intersection of climate policy, energy transition, and sustainable development.

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Economics and Management at Northwest A&F University in Xian, China. My research focuses on the economics of environmental sustainability, agricultural productivity, and climate adaptation.

My work spans a diverse range of topics including green finance, carbon regulation, energy security, ecological asset valuation, and agricultural technical efficiency. I employ advanced econometric techniques including panel causality, ARDL, GMM, and generalized additive models alongside emerging machine learning approaches for land-use and ecological security analysis.

Geographically, my research covers China, Nordic countries, BRICS nations, ASEAN, the United States, Pakistan, and Turkey, offering comparative insights into how different institutional and policy contexts shape environmental and economic outcomes.

I have authored or co-authored 18 peer-reviewed journal articles and actively contribute to the academic community as a reviewer for leading journals in environmental science, energy, and economics. You can explore my complete body of work in the publications section, or reach out via the contact form for collaborations and inquiries.
